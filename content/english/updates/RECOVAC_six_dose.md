The [RECOVAC (Register-based large-scale national population study to monitor COVID-19 vaccination effectiveness and safety) project](https://www.gu.se/en/research/recovac) is a register-based national population study that uses an observational research approach to monitor the effectiveness and safety of COVID-19 vaccinations. The RECOVAC project also evaluates the adverse effects of vaccination, and assesses whether they covary with other patient traits. It is led by **Prof. Fredrik Nyberg (University of Gothenburg)**.

The [RECOVAC dashboard](https://www.pathogens.se/dashboards/recovac/) on the Swedish Pathogens Portal is updated every time that the RECOVAC project receives a new data release from the registries. The dashboard contains information about the project, including the methodology used and how to request access to the data, as well as visualisations of some data from the project. In particular, the dashboard contains two visualisations showing:

- How the number of admissions to intensive care units (ICU) among adults in Sweden relates to the coverage of vaccination.
- How vaccination coverage relates to the number of COVID-19 cases amongst people with certain comorbidities (including cancer, cardiovasular disease, diabetes, and respiratory disorders).

In the **latest update, the RECOVAC project has provided data for up to 6 vaccine doses**. This allows a more comprehensive view of vaccine coverage in Sweden. Data from most registries has also been made available until the beginning of 2024. Please view the [RECOVAC dashboard](https://www.pathogens.se/dashboards/recovac/) to find out more about the project and/or to request access to the data from it.
